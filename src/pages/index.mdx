## Abstract

We study **personalized decision modeling** in settings where individual choices deviate from population-optimal predictions. We introduce **ATHENA**‚Äîan *Adaptive Textual‚Äësymbolic Human‚Äëcentric Reaso*ning framework‚Äîbuilt in two stages: (1) a **group‚Äëlevel symbolic utility discovery** procedure (LLM‚Äëaugmented symbolic search) learns compact, interpretable utility forms; and (2) an **individual‚Äëlevel semantic adaptation** step refines a **personal template** with TextGrad to capture each person‚Äôs preferences and constraints. On **Swissmetro** (travel mode choice) and **COVID‚Äë19 vaccine uptake** tasks, ATHENA consistently outperforms utility‚Äëbased, ML, and LLM baselines, improving F1 by ‚â• 6.5% over the strongest alternatives while maintaining calibrated probabilistic predictions. Together, these stages bridge classic **Random Utility Maximization** with **textual semantic context**, yielding interpretable structure and personalized reasoning.

### Two‚ÄëStage Pipeline

1. **Group‚Äëlevel symbolic utility discovery.** LLM‚Äëguided sampling + feedback (mutation/crossover) proposes candidate utility formulas; we select <LaTeX inline formula="f^*_g" /> by cross‚Äëentropy over held‚Äëout data.
2. **Individual‚Äëlevel semantic adaptation.** Seeded by <LaTeX inline formula="f^*_g" />, a **personalized semantic template** <LaTeX inline formula="P_i" /> is iteratively improved using **textual gradients** to minimize the individual loss.

### Why it works

- **Interpretable structure.** Symbolic utilities converge to compact, reusable fragments (e.g., age‚Äìtrust interactions), improving generalization and insight.
- **Personalization.** TextGrad adapts from a strong symbolic prior, avoiding local minima of unguided prompting. Ablations show either stage alone underperforms the full pipeline.
